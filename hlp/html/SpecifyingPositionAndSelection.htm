Specifying position and selection length

Position and length can be specified in one of three formats.

  1. Hours:minutes:seconds.miliseconds;
  2. Samples (with thousands comma-separated);
  3. Hours:minutes:seconds.frames, where frames are 75 per second (addressing used on compact disks);

Time position is shown in the dialogs and the status bar with one of those formats; besides, there is fourth format option: seconds.milliseconds.

In the selection and GoTo dialog boxes, you can choose one of the above formats.

If Samples option is in use, just enter the sample number, possibly comma-separated (commas are ignored).

If any other option is selected, you can enter hours, minutes and seconds, separated with semicolon, and milliseconds or frames, separated with the decimal point. You don't have to convert the time to the canonical form, with number of seconds and minutes less than 60. Such input as 100 (1 minute 40 seconds), or 80:50 (one hour 20 minutes 50 seconds) is valid, too.

If Hours:minutes:seconds.frames format is selected, whatever goes after the decimal point is considered number of frames. Otherwise, you should put 'f' after it, like:

79:50.45f
