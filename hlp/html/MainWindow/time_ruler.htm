Time Ruler

Time ruler is drawn above the wave or FFT view and shows the positions in the file.

As the mouse is moved over the ruler, the cursor becomes a dual-ended arrow. You can drag it to the left and right, while holding the left mouse button. The ruler and the file view will follow the cursor.

The time can be shown in HH:MM:SS.ms format (hours, minutes, seconds, miliseconds), or seconds, or as samples, or HH:MM:SS:frames (75 frames/s). To change the sound format, right click the ruler, and select the desired menu item:

The menu also allows to change time scale. Zoom In Full command sets 1:1 scale, to see the most details of the waveform. The keyboard shortcut for it is Ctrl+Num'+' ("plus" key on the numeric keypad).

You can also change the time representation, using View menu, Time ruler submenu.

On the left side of the time ruler, two buttons are shown:

The left button is for zooming in, the right one is for zooming out. Note: is the amplitude ruler is hidden, the buttons are not shown.

Keyboard shortcut for zooming in is '+' key on the numeric keypad.

Keyboard shortcut for zooming out is '-' key on the numeric keypad.

You can hide and show the time ruler by pressing Alt+5.
