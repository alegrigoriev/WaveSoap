Amplitude Ruler

Amplitude ruler is shown at the left side of the wave view.

If the sound is shown with vertical zoom (not the whole waveform fits in the window), you can see different parts by dragging the ruler up and down, while holding the mouse's left button.

The sound levels can be shown in percents, decibels, sample values. To change it, click on the ruler with the right mouse button and select from this menu:

If Sample Value is selected, the full range shown is from -32768 to 32767.

If Percents is selected, the full range shown is from -100% to 100%.

If dB is selected, maximum amplitude corresponds to 0 dB, every -6 dB make the amplitude about less in half.

You can also change the amplitude display mode, using View menu, Amplitude Ruler submenu.

On the lower end of the ruler, two buttons are shown:

The left button is for zooming in, the right one is for zooming out.

Keyboard shortcut for zooming in is Alt+Up Arrow.

Keyboard shortcut for zooming out is Alt+Down Arrow.

If you double click on the ruler, the zoom scale is reset back to 100%.

You can hide and show the time ruler by pressing Alt+6.
