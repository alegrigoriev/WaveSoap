Spectrum view

Spectrum view shows how the sound energy is distributed among different frequencies. The spectrum is calculated using FFT procedure, which stands for Fast Fourier Transform.

Lower side of each channel's display corresponds to lower frequencies; upper side corresponds to higher frequencies.

Greater power is shown with brighter red and orange colors, lower power is shown with darker blue colors.

Selected area is outlined with a wide dotted line.

The frequency is displayed on the left sidebar, at the same place where the amplitude ruler is shown for the waveform view.

Right-click in the spectrum view opens the popup menu. Besides from usual zoom commands, it allows to select number of FFT bands (also called bins) and type of FFT windowing function.

Number of FFT bins is power of 2, from 64 to 8192. More bins give better resolution in frequency domain, but lower resolution in time, because each FFT block takes more source audio samples.

Each block of source samples is shaped by a chosen FFT window function. Without such windowing, it's not possible to separate the frequency bands well enough, because energy from one band would bleed (leak) to other bands, far from it.

Window type can be chosen from the following:

Squared sine is also known as Hanning window. It has low leakage to the far bins, but the sidelobe has -30 dB leakage, which is a bit worse than Hamming window.

Hamming window (0.54 – 0.46*cos(2*pi * n/N)) has the lowest leak to the first bin, but the worst leakage to the far bins. All sidelobes of its transfer function are mere -40 dB.

Nuttall window: 0.355768 - 0.487396*cos(2*pi * n/N) + 0.144232*cos(4*pi * n/N) - 0.012604*cos(6*pi * n/N). This is so far the best window, but there may be reasons you want to select Hanning or Hamming window.

On the lower end of the ruler, two buttons are shown:

The left button is for zooming in, the right one is for zooming out.

Keyboard shortcut for zooming in is Alt+Up Arrow.

If you double click on the ruler, the zoom scale is reset back to 100%.

Keyboard shortcut for zooming out is Alt+Down Arrow.
